Live Nation

The Unplanned Premium Setup

A guest arrived in a wheelchair — unannounced, not my client, not flagged in advance. Before anyone asked me to do anything, I had already moved: table, tablecloth, lighting, menus, dedicated wait staff, front-of-stage placement. A premium accessible experience, built in real time, for someone who had no reason to expect it.

"Nobody assigned that to me. I did it because that's what the brand standard required — whether anyone was watching or not."

Live Nation

The Kid Who Couldn't Stay Up

A premium member's son couldn't attend his favorite artist's concert — school night, show started too late. I mobilized my GM, talent booker, marketing, and operations to arrange a private meet-and-greet before the show. Lanyard, autographs, merch, his favorite artist — just for him. He never heard a single song. He didn't need to.

"That's a core memory. That family has been loyal ever since. Events done right aren't forgotten — they're told as stories for years."

State National · Markel

The Baseball Box

Inherited a completely dark at-risk account. Key decision maker not responding to anything. I researched him, learned he loved baseball. My dad owned a sports store. I built a box of vintage memorabilia from his favorite team, sent it with a handwritten note. He called within the week. The account made it into the RFP.

"Individualization isn't a tactic — it's a philosophy. I bring it to every relationship I manage, internal or external."
